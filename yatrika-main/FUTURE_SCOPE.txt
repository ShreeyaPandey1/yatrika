While the current project is designed as a hackathon-ready safety solution, it has strong potential to grow into a large-scale intelligent public safety platform. One of the most promising future improvements is the integration of Social Network Analysis (SNA) to move from reactive emergency response to proactive risk detection.

In the proposed graph-based model, important nodes such as Tourist, Zone, Incident, Route, Hotel, Guide, Transport Point, eFIR, Panic Call, and Verification Event can be connected through meaningful relationships like visited, reported at, traveled through, linked to incident, repeated complaint at, and close spatiotemporal overlap with. By analyzing these relationships, the system can discover hidden movement patterns, repeated unsafe locations, suspicious overlaps between incidents, and high-risk travel paths. This would help authorities identify emerging danger zones earlier and improve resource deployment.

Another major future enhancement is an intelligent emergency escalation mechanism. If a tourist triggers a panic alert and the listed emergency contacts are called but do not pick up, the system can automatically escalate the case to the next response layer. With proper legal authorization, user consent, and privacy protection, authorities could be granted temporary access to nearby camera or CCTV feeds around the tourist’s last known location. This would provide real-time visual context, improve incident verification, and help emergency teams respond faster and more accurately.

Beyond this, the platform can be scaled with features such as predictive hotspot detection, safer route recommendations, repeated complaint tracking for specific zones or service providers, and stronger coordination between tourists, local authorities, hotels, and transport systems. Over time, the project can evolve from a hackathon prototype into a smart tourism safety network that not only responds to emergencies but also predicts and prevents them.
